# WORKED EXAMPLE — Martineau vs. Janvier

*FORMAT ILLUSTRATION ONLY. This example demonstrates the template's structure and voice — nothing more. The Parsers shown were not sourced under the Parser Sourcing Protocol and must not be treated as placements, precedents, or examples of how a Parser is identified. In live use, both Parsers arrive as user-declared placements before the read.*

## Pre-Match Intel

**Head-to-Head (H2H) Record:** 2–0 in favor of Matteo Martineau prior to this matchup (notable wins include a tight three-set battle at the Oeiras Challenger).

**Handedness:**
- **Matteo Martineau:** Right-handed (two-handed backhand)
- **Maxime Janvier:** Right-handed (two-handed backhand)

**Surface Breakdown & Struggles:**
- **Martineau:** Highly balanced on clay, but can struggle with depth control against extremely chaotic, big-serving flat hitters who deny him baseline entry rhythm.
- **Janvier:** Armed with an explosive, high-ceiling serve and massive groundstrokes, but historically struggles with extreme unforced error streaks and severe double-fault liabilities under sustained pressure on slow red dirt.

**Current Injuries:** None — neither competitor has active physical limitations or medical tape heading into this qualification final.

**Event Context:** ATP Challenger qualifying final, red clay, Lyon.

---

## Match Metrics

| Metric | Matteo Martineau (FRA) | Maxime Janvier (FRA) |
|---|---|---|
| Primary Court Surface | Clay / Indoor Hard Court | All-Court / Fast Surfaces |
| 2-Set Victory/Loss Pattern | ~65% of matches resolve in straight sets | High variance; prone to sudden straight-set mental collapses |
| 3-Set Victory/Loss Pattern | Extremely high efficiency; frequently extends past the 2-hour mark | Struggles to maintain physical/mental baseline depth across three sets |

---

## Parser Architecture Projection

### Player A: Matteo Martineau (FRA)
**Parser:** Balanced • Present • Self
**Tennis Behavior:** Calculated • Reactive • Force Game (attritional)

- **What becomes available first:** His own pacing and baseline rhythm. He dictates from his structural patterns outward.
- **What they are likely to do under pressure:** Extend rallies, lengthen the match, and drive up the physical cost on the opponent's stroke mechanics.
- **What they may miss:** A clean fast start when his rhythm is denied early by a flat hitter taking the ball out of his strike zone.
- **What conditions amplify their architecture:** Slow red clay, long matches past two hours, deciding sets that reward tactical discipline.
- **What conditions punish their architecture:** Chaotic big-serving flat hitters who deny baseline entry rhythm before he establishes pacing.

### Player B: Maxime Janvier (FRA)
**Parser:** Concrete • Present • Self (front-runner / momentum-dependent)
**Tennis Behavior:** Force Game • Vibing • High-ceiling first strike

- **What becomes available first:** His own explosive serve and groundstroke power — the first-strike opportunity.
- **What they are likely to do under pressure:** Over-press the low-margin flat shot; expose a fragile second serve and accumulate double faults under tension.
- **What they may miss:** The compounding physical cost of a lengthening match; he reads a hot start as durable rather than borrowed.
- **What conditions amplify their architecture:** Fast surfaces, short points, a high first-serve percentage (65%+), an early lead to buffer his error rate.
- **What conditions punish their architecture:** Slow red clay that multiplies his tracking cost, extended rallies, and maximum-tension sequences that trigger double-fault clusters.

---

## Winning Constants

- **Winning the 1st Set:** *Janvier* — absolutely vital to buy emotional real estate; a 6–4 first set cushions his high-error style. *Martineau* — usually dictates pacing; missing it forces an immediate defensive grind.
- **Front-Runner Reliability:** High for Janvier *only* when his first-serve percentage stays above 65%. Once that delivery dips, his closing confidence drops rapidly.
- **Unbeatable in Deciding Sets:** Martineau carries a vastly superior structural threshold in deciding sets, with greater tactical discipline on clay.
- **High First-Serve Performance:** Critical for Janvier to protect his baseline position and shield his highly vulnerable second delivery from Martineau's returns.

---

## Losing Trends

- **The "First Set" Rule:** Dropping the opener forces Martineau to extend rallies heavily, intentionally driving up the physical cost of Janvier's stroke mechanics.
- **Surface Struggle:** Red clay actively works against Janvier's flat-line striking, multiplying the tracking cost of his low-margin shots.
- **Tiebreak / Tension Trouble:** Janvier has a documented history of piling up double faults when sets reach maximum structural tension.
- **Loss of Momentum:** Janvier gives away massive game strings in clusters — mirrored by his sudden drop to 1–5 in this second set.
- **Pressure Cue:** With Martineau serving for the set at 5–1, the immediate psychological pressure sits entirely on Janvier to convert the active 30–40 break-point cue.
- **Statistical Trap:** Backing Janvier on his first-set win alone is deceptive; his architecture is fundamentally sequence-dependent and fragile against an opponent who lengthens the match.

---

## Collision & Live Frame Breakdown

**Live Frame Context:** Janvier ambushed the first set as a structural anomaly against the pre-match favorite. Martineau has reset and surged to 5–1 in the second, serving for the set with a live 30–40 break-point cue against him.

- **Player A (Martineau) is trying to:** Lengthen points and turn the match into a physical attrition test, using slow clay to break down Janvier's first-strike game.
- **Player B (Janvier) is trying to:** Recover a high first-serve rhythm to re-anchor his front-running confidence before the match length overrides his mechanics.

**The Predictable Misread:** Janvier is treating his first-set win as durable emotional capital rather than a borrowed lead on a punishing surface. He is waiting for his first-strike tennis to return instead of adjusting to the rising physical cost Martineau is imposing.

---

## Trajectory Resolution

- **The match favors Player A (Martineau) if:** Points stay long and physical. His Present/Self alignment lets him impose attrition without carrying the weight of losing the first set.
- **The match favors Player B (Janvier) if:** He restores a 65%+ first serve to shorten points and re-establish front-running rhythm before the deciding set arrives.

**Trajectory Call:** Martineau wins. A first-strike vs. attrition collision on a surface that punishes low-margin repetition. Janvier owns the archive of the first set, but Martineau owns the lengthening physical reality of the match. On slow clay the rally length breaks the front-runner — Janvier's architecture lacks a stable exit once his serve dips and the points stretch past his mechanical threshold.
