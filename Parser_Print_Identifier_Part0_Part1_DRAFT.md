# PART 0 — FOUNDATION

## 0.1 What a Parser Print Is

A Parser is not a personality type. It is not a cognitive style. It is not a preference.

A Parser is the orientation a person's reception system commits to before cognition begins — which portion of an incoming signal becomes available first. The orientation runs on three independent axes:

- **Spatial** — Concrete ↔ Balanced ↔ Abstract. Does the signal arrive as specifics or as structure?
- **Temporal** — Past ↔ Present ↔ Future. Does the signal anchor to what happened, what is happening, or what is forming?
- **Reference** — Self ↔ Balanced ↔ Other. Does the signal organize around the receiver, the other party, or the exchange itself?

A year of architecture work has shown one thing consistently: this orientation is a **cognitive fingerprint**. It expresses in every domain the person operates in — how they play, how they speak, how they prepare, how they decide — because it sits upstream of all of those expressions. The same print that shows in a tennis player's shot selection shows in their press conference verbs and their morning routine.

That is the claim this manual operationalizes: **one print, many expression surfaces.**

The current nine-bucket-per-axis-combination taxonomy (27 placements) is the ridge-class level. The print itself is finer — leanings within bins, intensity gradations, axis dominance orders. The buckets are where identification starts, not where it ends. Refinement beyond the buckets is reserved space in this framework: the taxonomy will grow as the recorded case base grows.

## 0.2 What This Manual Is

This is an **identification instrument**. It lists the language and behaviors by which each Parser orientation expresses, organized by domain, so that a trained reader can identify a person's print from observation — without an assessment instrument, without the person's cooperation, and without contaminating the read with conditions.

It exists to solve a specific problem. The CBI Tennis Parser Behavior Playbook (V2.1) Step 1 states: *Parser identity must come from outside the match. Do not infer it from match behavior alone — behavior under conditions can mimic any Parser.* This manual is the outside-the-match source. Cross-domain triangulation is what makes a placement trustworthy: a single match can mimic any Parser; a press conference, a practice routine, and a shot-selection pattern agreeing with each other cannot.

## 0.3 What This Manual Is Not

- It is not an assessment. The Parser Profile™ assessment remains the calibrated instrument. This manual is for subjects who cannot or will not take it.
- It is not a diagnostic tool. A print is not a condition, a disorder, a deficit, or a prognosis. (Edition C expands this boundary in the Clinical Application part.)
- It is not a skill or ability measure. Every print exists at every skill level. A print tells you how signal is received — never how well the person performs.
- It is not a behavior predictor in the deterministic sense. It predicts the grain of reception and the cost of certain environments, not specific actions.

## 0.4 Evidence Rules

Three rules govern what counts as a tell.

**1. Reflexive beats deliberate.**
The print lives in first responses — the first 30 seconds of an answer, the instinctive shot, the unplanned route home. Deliberate, rehearsed, or coached behavior is filtered through downstream layers (personality, training, social desirability) and can mimic any orientation. When reflexive and deliberate evidence conflict, weight the reflexive.

**2. Multi-context beats single-context.**
One domain can be masked by role demands (a point guard runs the system; a defendant follows the lawyer's script). A tell that repeats across independent contexts — sport AND speech AND habit — is print. A tell that appears in one context only is provisional until corroborated.

**3. Time-stable beats situational.**
Conditions pressure expression. Fatigue, score state, crisis, and novelty all bend behavior temporarily. A tell observed across months and across situations is print. A tell observed once under pressure is a condition response until it repeats.

## 0.5 Confidence Tiers

Every placement made with this manual carries a tier. Never report a placement without one.

- **PROVISIONAL** — tells from one domain only, or fewer than three independent observations. Usable for hypothesis only. Not case-study grade.
- **CORROBORATED** — two independent domains agree, no contradictions. Usable for working reads, flagged as such.
- **LOCKED** — three or more independent domains agree, zero unresolved contradictions, observations span time. Case-study grade. Only LOCKED placements enter the tracker as Parser inputs.

A contradiction does not average out — it blocks. If speech says Abstract and habits say Concrete, the placement stays PROVISIONAL until the contradiction is resolved (usually by discovering a role mask or a mis-scored tell). Forcing a placement through a contradiction is the single most common identification error.

## 0.6 Contamination Warnings

Four contamination sources defeat naive identification. Check all four before trusting any tell.

**Conditions mimicry.** Pressure can make any Parser look like any other for stretches. A Future player grinding out a defensive set looks Past. An Abstract player managing a lead looks Concrete. This is why single-match inference is banned in V2 — the same rule applies to single-observation inference in any domain.

**Role masks.** Positions, jobs, and social roles force behaviors regardless of native print. A therapist trained in reflective listening sounds Other-referenced in session regardless of print. The print shows in the residual — the off-script moments, the choices the role does not dictate.

**Coaching masks.** Trained media answers, rehearsed presentations, and drilled patterns are deliberate-layer behavior. Listen for the moment the script runs out.

**Social desirability.** People perform the orientation they believe is admirable — usually Balanced ("I see both sides") and Other ("it's about the team"). The performance collapses under fatigue, surprise, or low-stakes moments when no one seems to be watching. Those are the windows.

## 0.7 How to Use This Manual

1. Collect observations across at least two domains before scoring anything.
2. Score each axis **independently**. Never let a Spatial read pull the Temporal read.
3. Run the disambiguation pairs (Section 1.4) on any axis where two adjacent orientations both seem plausible.
4. Check the four contamination sources.
5. Assign the confidence tier.
6. Record the evidence, not just the conclusion — every placement should be auditable back to its tells.

# PART 1 — THE UNIVERSAL PRINT LAYER

*Everything in Part 1 is domain-independent. The domain chapters (Parts 2–4) map these signatures onto specific expression surfaces. If a domain chapter ever seems to conflict with Part 1, Part 1 governs.*

## 1.1 Spatial Axis — How Signal Arrives

### Concrete

**Core signature:** Signal arrives as specifics. The named thing, the actual instance, the confirmed fact. Structure is assembled later, from accumulated specifics, if at all.

**Universal tells:**
- Answers begin with instances, not principles: a story, a date, a name, an exact event.
- Comfort requires confirmation. New claims are tested against known cases before acceptance.
- Explanations build bottom-up: example, example, example, then (maybe) the rule.
- Discomfort with hypotheticals that have no anchor in something that has actually occurred.
- Precision vocabulary: *specifically, exactly, that day, the actual, in that case.*
- Trust in the demonstrated over the projected. "Show me" beats "imagine if."

**Under pressure:** Retreats to the confirmed. Repeats what has worked. Narrows to the known sequence.

### Balanced (Spatial)

**Core signature:** Signal arrives with both the instance and its frame, weighted by circumstance. Neither pole is home; the person moves between specifics and structure as the situation demands — genuinely, not as performance.

**Universal tells:**
- Oscillates visibly within a single answer: a principle illustrated immediately by a case, or a case lifted immediately to its principle.
- Asks scope questions before answering: "in what context?" "for what purpose?"
- Has exits. When one framing stalls, shifts to the other without losing the thread.
- Neither hypotheticals nor specifics produce discomfort; both are workable material.

**Under pressure:** Steps back to assess. Widens the frame before recommitting. (Contrast with Present-Temporal's *staying inside* the moment — see Disambiguation 1.4.)

**Authenticity check:** Genuine Balanced oscillates fluidly and unprompted. Aspirational Balanced (social desirability) commits to one pole and appends "but also..." as decoration. The appended pole carries no detail; the home pole carries all of it.

### Abstract

**Core signature:** Signal arrives as structure. The pattern, the principle, the shape of the thing. Specifics are instances of structure — interchangeable, sometimes barely retained.

**Universal tells:**
- Answers begin with what something *is about*, *means*, or *could become* — before (or instead of) what it literally is.
- Compresses cases into categories quickly, sometimes losing the case details entirely.
- Comfortable far from confirmation: speculates, extrapolates, builds on premises not yet established.
- Essence vocabulary: *essentially, fundamentally, the point is, what this really is, in principle.*
- Boredom with rehearsal of the known. Drawn to the unestablished edge of any topic.

**Under pressure:** Expands. Opens more possibility space rather than narrowing. Risk: opens futures the ground has not earned.

## 1.2 Temporal Axis — Where Signal Anchors

### Past

**Core signature:** Incoming signal is matched against the archive. What has already happened is the reference frame for what is happening.

**Universal tells:**
- Reaches for precedent first: "last time," "what we did before," "historically."
- Decisions cite track record — their own or others'. Proof means *it already worked.*
- Strong, detailed recall of prior events deployed as the natural argument form.
- New situations are processed by finding what they resemble.
- Verb anchor: past tense dominates spontaneous speech.

**Under pressure:** Repetition of the proven. Falls back on the recorded sequence. Strength when the situation repeats; cost when it doesn't.

### Present

**Core signature:** Signal is processed live. The current moment — its timing, texture, and immediate cues — is the reference frame. Not archive retrieval, not forward projection: raw now.

**Universal tells:**
- Speaks in current state: what is happening, how it feels, what's in front of them right now.
- Decisions track live cues — adjusts in real time, often unable to articulate why until afterward.
- Weak attachment to plans; strong attachment to the read of the moment.
- The past is referenced rarely and loosely; the future as a vague direction, not a structure.
- Verb anchor: present tense and present progressive dominate.

**Under pressure:** Goes deeper into the moment. Feel over plan. Strength when cues are clean; cost when the moment is noise.

### Future

**Core signature:** Signal is processed as trajectory. Where this is going is the reference frame for what it is. The person is already at the destination, waiting for the present to arrive.

**Universal tells:**
- Speaks in outcomes and trajectories: "where this leads," "what this sets up," "by next year."
- Acts before the thing fully arrives — commits early, positions ahead.
- Treats current state as raw material for the projected state. Mild impatience with the present's pace.
- Plans have structure and contingencies; the future is detailed in the way Past players' memories are detailed.
- Verb anchor: future constructions and conditionals dominate.

**Under pressure:** Commits earlier and harder. Accelerates toward the projected resolution. Strength when the projection is calibrated; cost when ground truth shifts under it.

## 1.3 Reference Axis — What Signal Organizes Around

### Self

**Core signature:** Signal organizes around the receiver's own state, capability, and intention. The first available question is *what can I make happen?*

**Universal tells:**
- First-person ownership of events: what *I* did, what *I* felt, what *I'm* building.
- Evaluates situations by their effect on own goals before (or instead of) their effect on others.
- Generates rather than reads: imposes a rhythm, an agenda, a style — and is often genuinely unaware of how it lands.
- Under-notices others' adjustments. Surprised to learn they were being read.
- Pronoun camera: *I/my* dominates; others appear as context for the self-narrative.

**Not selfishness.** Self-Reference is a reception orientation, not a moral position. Generous, devoted people carry Self prints; their generosity is *self-organized* — they give what they decide to give.

### Balanced (Reference)

**Core signature:** Signal organizes around the exchange itself — not the self's agenda, not the other's display, but what is happening *between*. The live interaction is the unit of attention.

**Universal tells:**
- Tracks the conversation/relationship/rally as a thing with its own state: "where are we," "what does this need."
- Shifts stance fluidly — leads when the exchange asks for it, yields when it asks for that — without either feeling like a strategy.
- Describes events as interactions rather than as my-actions or their-actions.
- Pronoun camera: *we/this/it* (the exchange) carries unusual weight.

**Authenticity check:** Same as Spatial-Balanced. Genuine Flow moves between stances unprompted and with full detail in both. Performed balance has a home stance and a decorated opposite.

### Other

**Core signature:** Signal organizes around the other party. The first available question is *what are you showing me?* The other's state, tendencies, and tells arrive before the self's agenda does.

**Universal tells:**
- Reads first, acts second. Opens by mapping the other person — mood, intent, pattern — and positions accordingly.
- Rich, accurate models of other people's habits, often more detailed than their model of themselves.
- Evaluates situations through others' eyes reflexively: "they'll think," "she needs," "what he's really doing."
- Own preferences surface late or on prompting.
- Pronoun camera: *you/they/he/she* dominates; the self appears as the responder.

**Not selflessness.** Other-Reference is where attention goes first, not a virtue. Manipulators carry Other prints; their manipulation is *other-organized* — built from reading.

## 1.4 Disambiguation Pairs

These are the lookalikes that destroy identification accuracy. When two adjacent orientations both seem plausible, run the specific separator. Do not place until the pair is resolved.

**The Flow vs Calculated (Reference-Balanced vs Spatial-Balanced).**
Both adapt. The Flow adapts *inside the live exchange* — present-tense engagement with what the interaction is becoming. Calculated adapts *in possibility space* — steps back, weighs the full situation, re-enters with a selection.
*Separator:* under pressure, The Flow stays in; Calculated steps out. Watch the pressure moment: continuous engagement = Flow; a visible beat of withdrawal-and-assessment = Calculated.

**Vibing speed vs Proactive speed (Present vs Future).**
Both act fast. The Present player is fast because they are reading live cues with no retrieval or projection latency. The Future player is fast because they committed before the cue arrived.
*Separator:* surprise the trajectory. Break the pattern mid-stream. The Present player adjusts *with* the break — their speed survives. The Future player's commitment lands where the target *was* — their speed becomes a miss. Also: ask them why they did it. Present says "it felt right / I saw it." Future says "I knew it was going there."

**Other-reading vs Flow-adapting (Other vs Reference-Balanced).**
Both respond to the other party. Other reads *the person* — builds a model of them and plays the model. Flow reads *the exchange* — tracks what the interaction needs, with the person as one input.
*Separator:* remove the person-pattern. Against an erratic or novel counterpart, Other degrades (no stable person to map); Flow doesn't (the exchange is still readable). In speech: Other talks about *them*; Flow talks about *it/this/us*.

**Concrete archive vs Past archive (Spatial-Concrete vs Temporal-Past).**
Both reach for the established. Concrete wants *confirmed specifics* — facts, regardless of when. Past wants *prior occurrences* — history, regardless of grain.
*Separator:* offer a confirmed abstract principle vs a vague past anecdote. Concrete takes the confirmed principle (it's verified, even though it's structural). Past takes the anecdote (it happened, even though it's loose). What they trust separates which axis is doing the work.

**Abstract projection vs Future projection (Spatial-Abstract vs Temporal-Future).**
Both live ahead of the given. Abstract opens *possibility structure* — what this could become as a shape, timeless. Future opens *trajectory* — where this is going as a timeline.
*Separator:* the presence of time. Abstract speculation has no clock ("imagine a version of this where..."). Future speculation always has one ("by the third set," "in two years"). Listen for whether the projection is dated.

## 1.5 Reading Axis Dominance

The three axes are independent, but in any given individual they are not equally loud. One axis typically expresses first and most often — the **lead axis**. Identifying the lead axis is the fastest route into a print, because the lead axis colors first responses across every domain.

- Lead-Spatial individuals open with what things *are* (grain).
- Lead-Temporal individuals open with *when* the relevant reality lives (anchor).
- Lead-Reference individuals open with *whose* frame is active (camera).

Score the lead axis by frequency of first-response type across observations. This ordering — lead, second, third — is part of the fine print structure beyond the 27 buckets, and is recorded on the Print Card even though the bucket taxonomy does not yet formalize it.
