## How to read this part

Every profile's tells are **derived from its block hierarchy** (profile-blocks.json), not written freehand. The chain is:

> Block hierarchy → brain region & function (per Mechanism Notes) → observable tell in each domain.

**HIGH blocks** produce the loud, reliable tells — what shows up. **LOW blocks** produce the *disconfirming* tells — what is reliably absent. The absence is as diagnostic as the presence: a profile is confirmed as much by what the person does NOT do as by what they do.

Each profile has four sections:
1. **Tennis Behavior** — on-court expression
2. **Speech Orientation** — language and press-conference expression
3. **Daily Habits** — off-court, daily-life expression
4. **Disambiguation** — the neighboring profile(s) most easily confused with this one, and the separator that tells them apart

Block → region map (from Mechanism Notes), used throughout:

| Block | Region | Function |
|---|---|---|
| Perceptual Discrimination (PD) | V1/V2, somatosensory | sub-200ms cue detection |
| Pattern Recognition (PR) | hippocampus, MTL | pattern completion, behavioral archive |
| Sequential Processing (SP) | pre-SMA/SMA | motor/tactical sequences across time |
| Spatial Relationships (SR) | posterior parietal | court geometry, body-in-space |
| Self-Reference Processing (SRP) | mPFC/PCC/DMN, LC-modulated | internal reference, composure |
| Social Signal Processing (SSP) | TPJ/STS/FFA | reading intent, social cues |
| Symbolic Manipulation (SM) | left IFG, angular gyrus | abstract/language-mediated reasoning |
| Generative Creation (GC) | DL-PFC + DMN | novelty, divergent output |

## SHARP — Concrete · Past · Self
*"The detail keeper who remembers everything specific"*

**HIGH:** Perceptual Discrimination · Sequential Processing · Self-Reference Processing
**MODERATE:** Pattern Recognition · Spatial Relationships
**LOW:** Generative Creation · Symbolic Manipulation · Social Signal Processing

### Tennis Behavior
- **PD:** Reads the ball clean and early off the strings; reaction defense is a strength; rewarded on faster surfaces.
- **SP:** Runs drilled, repeatable service and rally patterns; builds points in ordered steps; holds the sequence under fatigue.
- **SRP:** Steady internal state through score swings; imposes his own patterns rather than reacting.
- **LOW GC/SM (disconfirming):** Does NOT improvise novel shot-making — when the drilled pattern fails he runs it harder, not differently. Limited mid-match Plan B. Predictable once an opponent archives him, because he generates nothing new.
- **LOW SSP:** Doesn't read or exploit opponent tells; plays the ball and the pattern, not the person.
- **ID line:** Wins on clean early-ball detection and relentless drilled sequences with steady composure; identifiable by what's absent — no invention, no new plan, no playing the person.

### Speech Orientation
- Opens with specific instances: exact scores, dates, named opponents, what was literally said.
- Recounts in ordered sequence (SP in narrative form).
- First-person ownership of events (SRP).
- Precise sensory detail — surface, conditions, feel of contact (PD).
- **LOW SM/GC (disconfirming):** No metaphor, no analogy, no abstract principle; stays literal. Asked what a match *meant*, answers what *happened*. Markers: *specifically, exactly, that day, what happened was.* Absent: *essentially, fundamentally, the bigger picture.*
- **ID line:** Specifics, in order, from his own seat, no metaphor.

### Daily Habits
- Fixed routines in fixed order; sequence disruption is genuinely disruptive (SP).
- Proven methods — "this is how I've always done it" (Concrete-Past).
- Keeps records, logs, remembers exact amounts and placements (PD + Past).
- Internally referenced — organizes life around own confirmed systems (SRP).
- **LOW GC/SM (disconfirming):** Doesn't reinvent his methods; discomfort with open-ended, unprecedented, purely theoretical tasks; prefers concrete instructions over abstract principles.
- **ID line:** Same order, proven method, exact memory, own systems; visible discomfort with open-ended abstract tasks.

### Disambiguation
- **vs SEASONED (Concrete-Past-**Balanced**):** Same detail and archive. Separator is Reference — Sharp narrates from his own seat (*I did, I felt*); Seasoned narrates the situation as a whole, weighing his role against others'. Ask "what happened?" — Sharp gives his experience, Seasoned gives the event.
- **vs EMBODIED (Concrete-**Present**-Self):** Both somatic and self-anchored. Separator is Temporal — Sharp reaches for the archive ("last time, what worked before"); Embodied stays in the live moment ("right now, what I feel"). Sharp's verbs are past; Embodied's are present.
- **vs INTEGRATED (**Balanced**-Past-Self):** Both past-archived and self-referenced. Separator is Spatial — Sharp wants confirmed specifics; Integrated lifts the specific to its lesson. Offer an abstract principle: Sharp wants the concrete instance behind it; Integrated takes the principle.

## SEASONED — Concrete · Past · Balanced
*"The experience-grounded processor who learned the hard way"*

**HIGH:** Perceptual Discrimination · Sequential Processing · Pattern Recognition
**MODERATE:** Self-Reference Processing · Social Signal Processing
**LOW:** Generative Creation · Symbolic Manipulation

### Tennis Behavior
- **PD + SP:** Clean ball-reading plus drilled, ordered patterns — the veteran's repeatable game.
- **PR (high here, unlike Sharp):** Archives the opponent across the match and adjusts; the experience edge is real pattern-matching, not just composure.
- **MODERATE SRP/SSP:** Reasonable composure and some opponent-reading, neither dominant; plays the exchange's demands.
- **LOW GC/SM (disconfirming):** Still no improvised novelty or abstract re-planning — the adjustments are archive-driven, not invented.
- **ID line:** A grinder who reads the ball, runs proven patterns, AND archives the opponent to adjust — but adjusts from memory, never from invention.

### Speech Orientation
- Specifics and ordered recounting (PD + SP), but framed as *what the situation was* rather than purely *what I did* (Balanced reference).
- Draws lessons from accumulated experience: "you learn that…", "over the years…" (PR + Past).
- **LOW SM/GC:** Lessons stay concrete and experiential, not abstract or theoretical. Markers: *over time, you learn, what usually happens.*
- **ID line:** Experience stated as grounded specifics, framed situationally, no abstraction.

### Daily Habits
- Routines and proven methods (SP + Past), but adjusts them based on what experience has shown works across contexts (PR + Balanced).
- Reads situations through accumulated cases (PR).
- **LOW GC/SM:** Prefers the tested over the novel; not drawn to theoretical reinvention.
- **ID line:** Proven methods, experience-tuned, situationally framed.

### Disambiguation
- **vs SHARP (Concrete-Past-**Self**):** See Sharp entry — Reference axis (own seat vs whole situation), and Seasoned's higher PR means more genuine mid-stream adjustment.
- **vs GROUNDED (Concrete-**Present**-Balanced):** Separator is Temporal — Seasoned reaches for accumulated experience ("over the years"); Grounded deals with what's in front of him right now ("what's actually here"). Past lessons vs present pragmatism.
- **vs COHERENT (**Balanced**-Past-Balanced):** Separator is Spatial — Seasoned's wisdom is concrete and case-based; Coherent's is structural ("how things fit together"). Seasoned tells you the case; Coherent tells you the system.

## Cross-Profile Note: The Physical-Execution Gap

Five profiles share LOW scores across the physical-immediate cluster (Sequential Processing, Perceptual Discrimination, Spatial Relationships): **Sentimental, Visionary, Altruistic** (all three low), and to a lesser degree **Introspective, Reflective, Idealized, Mindful, Foresighted** (two of three low).

These are the **Abstract-family prints**. The pattern is mechanistically expected: the abstract-spatial orientation routes signal away from the concrete-immediate blocks toward Pattern Recognition, Symbolic Manipulation, and Generative Creation. In tennis specifically, this makes the Abstract family the hardest terrain — the sport rewards exactly the physical-immediate blocks these prints rank lowest. This is not a flaw in the print; it is the print expressing in a domain that taxes its low blocks. The same prints excel in domains that reward their high blocks (conceptual, generative, relational work). **Domain matters: a print that struggles in tennis can dominate in a field built on its high blocks.** This is the core reason identification must triangulate across domains rather than reading athletic performance alone.
